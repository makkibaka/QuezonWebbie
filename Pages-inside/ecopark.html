Description

La Mesa Ecopark is a public park located in Greater Lagro, Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. It covers an area of 33 hectares (82 acres) and is situated along the natural boundary of the La Mesa Watershed Reservation. The park offers a peaceful and lush environment, providing a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities such as biking, fishing, and boating. It's a great place to unwind and connect with nature.

History

The La Mesa Ecopark was rehabilitated in 1999 and officially opened to the public in 2004. The park was established outside the natural boundaries of the La Mesa Watershed and has since become a popular venue for outdoor recreation. Previously, the area was known as the La Mesa Park and Resort, which was established in the 1950s. However, due to neglect and disrepair, the park was eventually closed in the mid-1970s. Thanks to the efforts of restoration, the La Mesa Ecopark is now a thriving green space for people to enjoy.
