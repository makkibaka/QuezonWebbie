Quezon City

A diverse and flavorful culinary scene that reflects the rich tapestry of Filipino cuisine. As a melting pot of cultures, its food offerings blend traditional Filipino dishes with international flavors, showcasing a wide array of delectable treats and local specialties that cater to diverse palates and preferences.

  • Adobo : A quintessential Filipino dish, often made with chicken or pork, cooked in a marinade of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices. Quezon City's eateries offer variations of this beloved dish, each with its unique twist.
  • Halo-halo : A popular dessert especially cherished in the tropical climate, consisting of shaved ice topped with various sweet ingredients such as beans, fruits, jellies, ice cream, and evaporated milk. Different restaurants and dessert shops in Quezon City offer their versions of this refreshing treat.
  • Sisig : Originating from Pampanga, sisig has become a Filipino favorite. It's a sizzling dish made from chopped pig's face and ears, seasoned with chili peppers, calamansi, and onions. Quezon City's eateries serve up their versions of this flavorful dish, often with a modern twist.
  • Lechon : A festive dish of roasted whole pig, known for its crispy skin and succulent meat. Various restaurants and specialty shops in Quezon City offer this delicacy, often serving it during celebrations and gatherings.
  • Kare-Kare : A stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables cooked in a thick, savory peanut sauce. This dish is a staple in Filipino cuisine and can be found in many restaurants and eateries across Quezon City.
  • Tokwa't Baboy : A popular street food comprising fried tofu (tokwa) and pork (baboy) served with a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, onions, and chili. It's a flavorful snack available in many local food stalls and eateries.
  • Pancit Malabon : A noodle dish from Malabon City, adjacent to Quezon City, known for its thick rice noodles topped with a rich shrimp sauce, various seafood, boiled eggs, and fresh vegetables.
  • Street Food Delights : Quezon City offers a wide variety of street food such as fish balls, kwek-kwek (deep-fried quail eggs), barbecue skewers, and isaw (grilled chicken or pork intestines), which are popular among locals and visitors alike.
  • Local Markets and Delicacies : Farmers' markets and food bazaars in Quezon City offer an array of local delicacies, including longganisa (Filipino sausage), kesong puti (white cheese), and bibingka (rice cake).

Quezon City's food scene embodies the essence of Filipino culinary heritage, presenting a delightful fusion of flavors, textures, and aromas that cater to the diverse tastes of its residents and visitors.
