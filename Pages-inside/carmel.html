Description

The Carmel Shrine, formally known as the Minor Basilica of the National Shrine of Our Lady of Mount Carmel, is a Roman Catholic minor basilica and national shrine located in Barangay Mariana, Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. It is dedicated to Our Lady of Mount Carmel and holds a special place of veneration for the faithful. The shrine features beautiful architecture and provides a serene environment for prayer and reflection.

History

The Carmel Shrine has a rich history that dates back to its opening in 1964 after a decade of construction. It has become a significant religious landmark in Quezon City and is known for its Marian devotion rooted in the 1600s. The shrine serves as a place of worship, pilgrimage, and spiritual nourishment for the Catholic community. In 2020, it was officially designated as a national shrine, further cementing its importance in the religious landscape of the Philippines.
