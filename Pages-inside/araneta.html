Description

The Araneta Coliseum, also known as "The Big Dome," is a renowned indoor arena located in Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. It boasts a seating capacity of approximately 16,500 people, making it one of the largest indoor arenas in Asia. The coliseum is recognized for its distinctive dome-shaped roof and has become an iconic landmark in the area.

History

The Araneta Coliseum was officially inaugurated on March 16, 1960. It was built by the Araneta family, who envisioned creating a world-class venue for sports, entertainment, and other major events. The coliseum quickly gained popularity and became a hub for various activities.

Over the years, the Araneta Coliseum has hosted numerous significant events. It has been the venue for international basketball games, boxing matches featuring renowned fighters like Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier, volleyball tournaments, and ice skating shows. Additionally, it has been the stage for the annual Binibining Pilipinas beauty pageant, where the country's representatives for international pageants are selected.

The coliseum's central location and impressive facilities have made it a preferred choice for both local and international events. It continues to be a vibrant and dynamic venue, attracting a wide range of audiences and performers.
