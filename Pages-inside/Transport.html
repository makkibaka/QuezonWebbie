Quezon City

A bustling metropolitan hub in the Philippines, boasts a diverse transportation network that caters to the needs of its residents and visitors. Its transportation system encompasses various modes of travel, including public transportation, road networks, and alternative transport options, contributing to the city's connectivity and accessibility.

  • Jeepneys : Iconic to Philippine transportation, colorful jeepneys ply numerous routes within Quezon City and neighboring areas. These modified jeeps serve as the primary mode of public transport, offering affordable fares and covering various destinations.
  • Buses : Several bus terminals and routes connect Quezon City to different parts of Metro Manila and nearby provinces. Both city and provincial buses operate within the city, providing options for commuters traveling to different destinations.
  • MRT (Metro Rail Transit) and LRT (Light Rail Transit) : The MRT-3 and LRT Line 2 serve commuters within Quezon City, providing a rapid transit system that connects key areas within the city and links to other parts of Metro Manila. These trains offer a faster mode of transport, especially during peak hours.
  • Taxis and Ride-Hailing Services : Taxis and app-based ride-hailing services like Grab and Angkas are available for more convenient and private transportation options within Quezon City. They offer flexible travel for commuters and tourists.
  • Tricycles : Widely used for short-distance travel within barangays (neighborhoods) and areas with narrower streets, tricycles serve as a convenient means of transport for residents, especially in areas not easily accessible by larger vehicles.
  • Road Networks : Quezon City has an extensive road network, including major thoroughfares like EDSA (Epifanio de los Santos Avenue), Commonwealth Avenue, and Quezon Avenue. These roads connect the city to other parts of Metro Manila and facilitate the flow of traffic.
  • Bicycle Lanes and Alternative Transport Initiatives : Efforts to promote alternative transport modes have led to the creation of bicycle lanes in certain areas of Quezon City. Bike-sharing programs and pedestrian-friendly zones aim to encourage eco-friendly modes of travel and reduce traffic congestion.
  • Transport Terminals and Hubs : The city hosts various transport terminals and hubs, facilitating intercity and provincial travel. These terminals serve as points of departure and arrival for buses, jeepneys, and other modes of transport.
  • Traffic Management and Infrastructure Development : Ongoing initiatives focus on improving traffic management, upgrading transportation infrastructure, and implementing solutions to address the city's transportation challenges.

Quezon City's transportation system continues to evolve, aiming to provide efficient and accessible mobility options for its residents while adapting to the growing demands of a dynamic urban environment.
