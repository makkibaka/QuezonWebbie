Quezon City

City celebrates a vibrant tapestry of cultural diversity through its array of annual events and festivals. These gatherings serve as vibrant platforms for showcasing Filipino traditions, arts, cuisine, and community engagement. From religious celebrations to cultural showcases, these events bring together residents and visitors alike, adding vibrancy and color to the city's social calendar.

  • Quezon City Day : Celebrated annually on October 12, this event marks the city's founding anniversary. It features various activities, including parades, cultural shows, street fairs, and sports events, showcasing the city's history and achievements.
  • Panagbenga Festival : While not exclusive to Quezon City, the Panagbenga Festival, held in nearby Baguio City, is a popular event attended by Quezon City residents. It's a flower festival featuring grand parades, street dancing, and floral floats, celebrating the blooming of flowers and the city's culture.
  • Pahiyas Festival : Though originating from Lucban, Quezon Province, this festival attracts many Quezon City residents due to its colorful and festive nature. Celebrated every May 15, it involves decorating houses with colorful agricultural produce, particularly showcasing the well-known kiping (leaf-shaped rice wafers) as decorations.
  • International Silent Film Festival : Held annually, this event celebrates the art of silent films from various countries. It features screenings of classic silent movies accompanied by live music, fostering appreciation for cinematic history and cultural exchange.
  • Quezon City International Pink Film Festival : Celebrated in line with LGBTQ+ Pride Month, this festival showcases LGBTQ+ films, documentaries, and other related activities. It promotes awareness, acceptance, and equality for the LGBTQ+ community.
  • Feast of Sto. Domingo : A religious festival celebrated in honor of Saint Dominic de Guzman, the patron saint of the Sto. Domingo Church. It includes religious processions, feasting, and cultural activities, drawing devotees and spectators from different parts of the city.
  • Various Barangay and District Festivals : Quezon City's barangays (neighborhoods) and districts host their own fiestas and festivals, highlighting local culture, traditions, and history through street parades, beauty pageants, food fairs, and live performances.
  • Christmas Celebrations : The city comes alive during the holiday season with various Christmas-related events, including parades, light displays, bazaars, and concerts, fostering a festive atmosphere for residents and visitors.

These events and festivals in Quezon City serve as platforms for cultural exchange, community engagement, and the celebration of various facets of Filipino heritage, fostering a sense of unity and pride among its residents.
