Description

The University of the Philippines (UP) is a state university system in the Philippines and is considered the country's national university. It was established on June 18, 1908, and is mandated by Republic Act No. 9500 (UP Charter of 2008), which grants it institutional autonomy. UP offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs in various fields of study, including the sciences, humanities, social sciences, engineering, business, and the arts. It is known for its academic excellence, research contributions, and cultural impact. UP has multiple campuses across the Philippines, with the main campus located in Diliman, Quezon City.

History

The University of the Philippines was founded on June 18, 1908, through Act No. 1870 of the Philippine Assembly. It was the result of the recommendation of Secretary of Public Instruction, William Morgan Shuster, to the Philippine Commission. Since its establishment, UP has played a vital role in the development of higher education and research in the country. Over the years, it has produced notable alumni who have excelled in various fields, contributing to the advancement of knowledge and nation-building.
