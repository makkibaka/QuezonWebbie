Description

Sining Kamalig Art Gallery is an advocacy gallery located in Quezon City, Philippines. It offers art restoration services and other artistic services. The gallery showcases changing exhibitions featuring both recognized and upcoming Filipino artists. Sining Kamalig aims to make art accessible to everyone, regardless of age or nationality. They provide a democratic space for local artists to express their art and contribute to the art scene in the Philippines.



History

Founded in the early 1970s by sisters Vida Balboa and Corito Kalaw. The gallery was established with the goal of providing a platform for local artists to showcase their works and contribute to the development of the Philippine art scene. Over the years, Sining Kamalig has evolved from a traditional gallery space to a pop-up gallery that collaborates with various locations in Metro Manila. This allows them to reach a wider audience and bring art closer to the community. Today, Sining Kamalig continues to support and promote Filipino artists, offering a space for artistic expression and appreciation.
