Description

Art in Island is the largest Mixed Media Art Museum in Quezon City, Philippines. It offers a unique and interactive art experience where visitors can see and experience various art forms with light displays, projections, and installations. The museum showcases a mesmerizing and surreal world of mixed media through its exhibits. It combines art, media, and technology to create an immersive and engaging environment for visitors to explore and appreciate various art forms.

History

Founded in November 2014 by South Korean businessman Yun Jae Kyoung. Recognizing Filipinos' love for taking selfies and sharing them on social media, he created an interactive art exhibition that would cater to this trend. The initial exhibits were completed within four months by 18 Korean visual artists. Since its opening, Art in Island has gained popularity for its unique approach to art, featuring 3D paintings and optical illusions that allow visitors to become a part of the artwork. In December 2022, the museum reopened with new digital displays and was renamed "Art in Island: The Media Square," continuing to provide an immersive and interactive art experience for visitors.
